Parokya ni Edgar

Parokya ni Edgar is a Filipino rock band formed in 1993 in Quezon City, Philippines. The band is known for their humorous and often satirical songs, which often touch on social and political issues. The band's name is a play on words, as "parokya" means "parish" in Filipino, while "Edgar" is a common Filipino name.

Mayonnaise

The band were named after the song "Mayonaise" by The Smashing Pumpkins and were formed in 2002 with a number of earlier former members. They were launched into the mainstream in 2004 after winning the Grand Prize at the Red Horse Muziklaban 2004 as a four-piece lineup.

Rocks teddy

Filipino rock band signed on 12 Stone Records. Formerly known as Acoustic Faith, Rocksteddy was founded in March 2003 by fellow church members, a trio of Christian rockers including singer Teddy Corpuz, drummer Jeff Cucullo, and rhythm guitar Howie Ramos. In 2005, they decided to drop the Christian lyricism and acoustic style and turned to alternative rock.

Gloc9

Gloc-9 is a Filipino rapper and hip hop artist, whose real name is Aristotle Pollisco. Born in 1975 in Tondo, Manila, Gloc-9 started rapping as a hobby while working as an engineer. Through his music, he has established himself as one of the most respected hip hop artists in the Philippines.

Flow G

Known professionally as Flow G, is a Filipino rapper, singer and songwriter. He rose to prominence as a member of the Filipino hip-hop collective Ex Battalion and for hit solo releases "Rapstar", "Praning", and "Araw-Araw Love". He won the Wish Artist of the Year on the 9th Wish Music Awards in 2024

Moira Dela Torre

Moira Dela Torre is a Filipina singer-songwriter who rose to fame after joining the first season of the singing competition "The Voice of the Philippines" in 2013. Although she did not win the competition, she gained popularity for her soulful voice and unique musical style.
